# Rotation

## Single Target

**What is a priority list?**

When reading the priority lists below, you should not think about these as steps to follow in a specific order. At any given point in combat, you should cast the first thing in the list that you are able to cast.

The way this section is written is very formulaic and has the same structure as the Action Priority List (APL) used in sims.

**Pre-Combat:**

1. If you have it, precast [Algethar Puzzle Box](https://www.wowhead.com/item=193701/algethar-puzzle-box?class=11&hero=22&ilvl=289&spec=103)
2. !Prowl!
3. !Rake! from stealth.

<details>
<summary>Top Gate Explained</summary>

!Rejuvenation!: Does too little healing and takes you out form

!Wild Growth!: Better healing, but not enough to justify leaving form. It is not usable for defensive purposes as its a smart heal that does not care about your target.

!Ironfur!: Most damage we take as dps role is magical or pierces armor. In the times its not, !Frenzied Regeneration! and !Bear Form! are already enough.

!Sunfire!: Doesn't do much damage and can't use in !Cat Form!

!Starfire!: into Moonkin Form: Incredibly niche for times where you need access to moonkin !Wild Charge! or more rarely, !Flap!. In theory also provides a slight amount of damage if you are stuck out of melee with no other recourse.

!Hibernate!: Rarely, and almost always in dungeons, you can use this to remove a mob from a pack temporarily. Occasionally this can cause packs that path around to stop moving as it doesn't put you in combat.

!Maim!: An expensive stun in a world where AoE stuns are prevalent.

!Remove Corruption!: You will pretty much always talent this, but if you don't need to dispel anything it is droppable for the niche choices.

!Nurturing Instinct!: This increases the damage of Lunar Inspiration !155580|Moonfire! by a small amount if you have that talented. This isn't a particularly good talent otherwise but you can run out of things to choose that does something.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Middle Gate Explained</summary>

!Starlight Conduit!: Technically buffs convoke !Wrath! but its such a small amount of damage that you should consider it zero value.

!Lingering Healing!: This benefit is far too minor. In theory it synergizes with Forestwalk; in practice, if you need more healing when Regrowth is about to fall off, you just cast it again.

!Astral Influence!: Primarily used to increase the range of Lunar Inspiration !155580|Moonfire!. Other uses are hyper-niche.

!Matted Fur!: This effect got doubled and is thus a much more intriguing take. Essentially if you run out of other stuff, this is where you dump your points.

!Typhoon!: Displacement often finds a use in Raid. In keys its typically used as a soft-kick.

!Ursol's Vortex!: Often paired with !Typhoon! as a poor-man's sigil of chains. Excellent for kiting mobs or helping your tank do so. Particularly effective against mobs who fixate targets.

!Mass Entanglement!: AoE root. For whenever you need more roots than you can already cast via !Entangling Roots!.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Bottom Gate Explained</summary>

!Symbiotic Relationship!: This is severely undertuned. Never talent into it.

!Ursine Vigor!: Essentially limited to only m+. Its only use is to let you survive one-shots you otherwise wouldn't have. Typically only relevant in the highest key levels.

!Gale Winds!: Rare, and essentially only in raid, for when you need to make Typhoon more reliably knock all of its intended targets.

!Incessant Tempest!: Rare, and almost always in Raid, for when you need to knock twice in less than 30 seconds. 

!Lore of the Grove!: If you talent !Lunar Inspiration!, you should probably talent this as well.

!Perfectly-Honed Instincts!: Nets you more Well-Honed Instincts procs, which often come at a good time.

!Incapacitating Roar!: In keys this is required. AoE soft-kick, doesn't move mobs around or anything, it just works. Use !Fluid Form! to return back to !Cat Form!.

!Fluid Form!: This is great quality of life and saves you GCDs whenever you find yourself shifting out of !Cat Form!. Particularly nice in keys to go along with !Incapacitating Roar! and !Bear Form!.

!Improved Stampeding Roar!: If you need nothing else, or if your raid leader tells you to talent into this.

!Lycara's Inspiration!: An alternate defensive option for the 4% stamina. I consider this weaker than !Forestwalk! but sometimes you have the free points, and other times you may just feel the increased health is more valuable than the healing taken increase.

!Heart of the Wild!: An extra defensive (bear) most of the time. That being said, the Wild Growth (caster) version is a solid amount of healing too, about 1.5 times your health pool split across 5 injured players in your group. Its healing is frontloaded too, so it has life-saving potential.

!Innervate!: If your healer demands it of you.

</details>

## Crafting

**Crafting Order:**

Firstly, you can craft a veteran piece gear without using a spark, and place an embellishment on it. I'd recommend that you craft 246 Bracers with [Arcanoweave Lining](https://www.wowhead.com/item=240166/arcanoweave-lining).

With your sparks:
- [Aln'hara Pikestaff](https://www.wowhead.com/item=245771/alnhara-pikestaff?bonus=12214:12497:12066:12693:8960:8791:13622:13667&class=11&crafted-stats=32:49&crafting-quality=8&hero=22&ilvl=285&spec=103) (or any crafted 2h agi weapon) with [Darkmoon Sigil: Hunt](https://www.wowhead.com/item=245875/darkmoon-sigil-hunt). If you already have a myth track weapon, you can skip this.
- Sim the best slots for your gear using [Arcanoweave Lining](https://www.wowhead.com/item=240166/arcanoweave-lining)
- An alternative, if the best slot for your gear is to craft a ring, is [Loa Worshiper's Band](https://www.wowhead.com/item=251513/loa-worshipers-band?bonus=8960:12214:12066:9627&hero=22&ilvl=285&spec=103). This ring is virtually identical damage wise to [Arcanoweave Lining](https://www.wowhead.com/item=240166/arcanoweave-lining) which, notably, cannot be crafted on rings.

If you're a mythic raider, you can choose to gamble on acquiring a myth track weapon early (2h polearm drops from 2nd boss: [Inescapable Reach](https://www.wowhead.com/item=249302/inescapable-reach?bonus=13335:12806)), and instead upgrade a hero track weapon to 6/6 for now. This is more efficient crest wise in the long run, but can lead to slower progression by missing out on the early power boost if your gamble fails.

Endgame ideal is on Wrists + Cloak/Loa Worshipers Band, but you should try to get your item level as high as you can in all slots.

# Miscellaneous

## What is snapshotting?

Snapshotting is when a damage-over-time (DOT) effect benefits from certain buffs based on how things were when it was applied, rather than dynamically adjusting to the current state. It takes a "snapshot" of those buffs whenever you apply the dot.

Note: You will not necessarily upgrade every snapshot.

__Which bleeds snapshot which buffs?__

| DoT | !Tiger's Fury! | !Pouncing Strikes! |
| :-: | :-: | :-: |
| !Rake! | 	✅ | ✅ |
| !Rip! | ✅ | ❌ |
| !Primal Wrath! | ✅ | ❌ |
| !Lunar Inspiration! | ✅ | ❌ |
| !Dreadful Wound! | Only Upgrades | ❌ |
| !Feral Frenzy! | Each direct hit separately | ❌ |

> [!TIP]
> !Pouncing Strikes! can be applied via !58984|Shadowmeld! or !Prowl!

A dot will ideally be applied only when 30% or less of the prior dot remains. This triggers a mechanic known as “pandemic,” which is common to most damage over time spells.

## What is “pandemic range?”
This term refers to a DoT mechanic in WoW. The baseline duration of a DoT can be extended by up to 30% if a target is already affected by that DoT. Due to the 30% cap on extension, DoTs generally shouldn't be refreshed too early, as potential ticks will be lost. The ideal window of time to refresh a DoT is known as its “pandemic range.”

Let’s use an example. Consider a 10 second DoT:
* Refresh with 1s remaining: the new DoT will be 11s long (10s baseline duration + 1s from prior DoT)
* Refresh with 3s remaining: the new DoT will be 13s long (10s baseline duration + 3s from prior DoT)
* Refresh with 8s remaining: the new DoT will still be 13s long (10s baseline + up to 30% from prior DoT)

It is therefore most efficient to wait until 70% or more of your bleed has ticked to refresh its DoT.

Fun trivia: the name “pandemic” derives from an old warlock passive that was eventually integrated as a baseline feature of damage over time spells.

__What is “clipping?”__

Clipping refers to refreshing a dot prior to its pandemic window.

## Macros

Here, we provide a list of helpful macros. These macros are not required, but may be of use to you. Customize them as you see fit.

**Cast Ursol's Vortex at your cursor location (@cursor macro):**

```
#showtooltip
/cast [@cursor] Ursol's Vortex
```

**Use your trinket(s) and/or weapon at the same time as an ability:**

Some trinkets and weapons should be used with a major cooldown. A lot of players will bind these items with Berserk.
* 13 is the top trinket slot
* 14 is the bottom trinket slot
* 16 is your weapon slot

If you would like to use all three items with Berserk, use this macro:

```
#showtooltip
/use 13
/use 14
/use 16
/cast Berserk
```
This macro may be modified. For example, the macro below only uses the 2nd trinket with Berserk:

```
#showtooltip
/use 14
/cast Berserk
```

**Shadowmeld into Rake** 

Need to press twice for it to work. If you press it once, it will only Shadowmeld (which you will want to do sometimes)

```
#showtooltip
/castsequence [nostealth] reset=5 Shadowmeld, Rake
```

**Cast Entangling Roots on your mouseover target:**

This macro casts Entangling Roots on your mouseover target. If your mouse is not hovering over a target, the macro instead casts Entangling Roots on your current target.
```
#showtooltip
/cast [@mouseover, harm, nodead] [] Entangling Roots
```

**Dispel/Soothe on the same bind:**

This is the classic help/harm macro, but using mouseovers as well.

```
#showtooltip
/cast [@mouseover, harm, nodead] Soothe; [@mouseover, help, nodead] Remove Corruption; [harm, nodead] Soothe; Remove Corruption
```

**Skull Bash your Focus target:**

This macro casts Skull Bash on your focus target. If you have not set a focus target, the macro instead casts Skull Bash on your current target.

We recommend creating a keybind that will assign a target as your focus with one click. This option can be found in the game's keybind settings.
```
#showtooltip
/cast [@focus, harm, nodead] [] skull bash
```

**Rebirth/Motw in one button:**

This macro casts Rebirth on your current friendly but dead mouseover target. If your ally is alive, the macro instead casts Mark of the Wild.
```
#showtooltip
/cast [@mouseover, help, dead] Rebirth; Mark of the Wild
```

**Why does my mouseover macro not work?**

You probably have it bound to a mouse button, which doesn't allow mouseover macros by default. To fix this, you can use an addon like Clicked, or just map the mouse-button to a different key. Backtick (`)/Tilde (~) can also have this issue. If you don't have it bound to a mouse-button and it still doesn't work, then there is likely an issue with the macro itself. In this case, it's almost always the ommission of help, harm or exists in the brackets containing mouseover.
